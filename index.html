Presentation

Who are we ?

In 2004 a group of engineering students and a teacher funded a club within ISI to promote free software.

After years of working in and out of our institute, Freeways evolved in 2014 from a club to an association.

Now we continue the mission just in a bigger scale.

Our principales

Freedom in computing is as crucial as freedom of speech.

Freedom to study, use, modify and distribute a program.

Respect the user's privacy.

Our visions

Promote free software and open hardware.

Unite students around the philosophy of Free Software.

Getting information with ease.

Our mission

Compete in national and international events.

Organise events to gather the Free Software community.

Make workshops around free/open technologies.

Contribute to open/free projects.

Some definitions

What is free software / open hardware ?

“Free as in Freedom”

By definition a free software must follow the 4 freedoms set by the Free Software Foundation.

But basacilly it's making the code available for the users.

The same for open hardware, but instead of sharing source code we share designs.

What is a proprietary software ?

Proprietary or closed-source software is software that is owned by an individual or a company.

The owner deprives users from all their freedoms.
